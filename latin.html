Ecclesiastical Latin

Begin with words already prayed.

Latin is not a test of whether somebody is a serious Catholic. It is one route into the language, music and memory of the Church.

The first prayer

The Sign of the Cross is short enough to learn as a whole. Its grammar also reveals something important: Catholic prayer begins in the name—not the names—of the Father, Son and Holy Spirit.

Latin

In nomine Patris,
et Filii,
et Spiritus Sancti.
Amen.

English

In the name of the Father,
and of the Son,
and of the Holy Spirit.
Amen.

Phrase by phrase

In nomine
In the name.
Patris
Of the Father.
et Filii
And of the Son.
et Spiritus Sancti
And of the Holy Spirit.
Amen
Truly; so be it.

Approximate pronunciation

In NO-mi-neh PAH-tris, et FEE-lee-ee, et SPEE-ree-toos SAHNK-tee.

An English approximation can only begin the work. Later lessons should include competent audio, complete prayers, plain grammar and modern explanations alongside established English forms.

Where this path can lead

The next stages will move through the Our Father, Hail Mary, Glory Be, Salve Regina, Rosary mysteries and Apostles’ Creed. The aim is not to collect phrases, but to understand and pray complete texts.
