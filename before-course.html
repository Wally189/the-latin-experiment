The grammar words

What they mean in ordinary English.

Noun
A person, place, thing or idea: Mary, Rome, a rose, faith.
Verb
An action or state: carries, sees, is, prayed.
Case
The form of a noun that helps show its job in a sentence. English keeps a small trace of this when he becomes him.
Declension
A family of nouns that changes its endings in a similar pattern.
Ending
The final part of a Latin word often gives a clue to what that word is doing.
Macron
A line over a vowel, such as ā, showing that the vowel is long.
The first sentence pattern

Doer → action → receiver

Maria carried a rose.
Maria did the carrying. Carried is the action. A rose received the action.
1

The doer nominative

Who or what performs the action.

2

The action verb

What happens or is true.

3

The receiver objective / accusative

The person or thing directly affected by the action.

Ecclesiastical pronunciation

A first reading guide.

Read slowly and clearly. These are the main sounds needed to begin.

C

Before e, i, ae or oe, usually sounds like ch.

G

Before e or i, usually sounds soft, as in gem.

GN

Sounds roughly like ny in canyon.

SC

Before e or i, usually sounds like sh.

V

Sounds like the English v.
