The course behind the experiment

Father Most provides the route.

After looking for an accessible Ecclesiastical Latin path, I identified Father William Most’s Latin by the Natural Method. The experiment asks whether I can complete it—and whether an honest companion can help another beginner attempt the same journey.

Principal source

Latin by the Natural Method

Father William Most’s method begins with connected Latin and introduces grammar through what the learner is already reading. That appealed to me more than beginning with pages of tables detached from meaning.

I am using his visible lesson numbering and sequence. TLE does not rename his lessons or imply ownership of the course.

Why it seemed appropriate

Reading first, grammar in context.

I wanted to test whether Latin could begin as language rather than as a wall of terminology. Father Most’s approach gives the learner sentences early and asks them to notice patterns before explaining every system at once.

That does not mean the terminology disappears. My first experience showed exactly where a complete beginner may need someone to pause and explain words such as nominative, case and declension.

What TLE adds

  • my first-person learning journal;
  • plain-English explanations of obstacles I actually met;
  • original, clearly labelled consolidation practice;
  • British-English pronunciation support;
  • a learner-owned notebook and progress record;
  • Catholic context where it genuinely helps.
Copyright and authorship boundary

The full lessons do not belong to TLE.

What the prototype does

It names and links the source, discusses the method, uses short quotations only when genuinely necessary, and provides original explanation, reflection and practice.

What it does not do

It does not place Father Most’s complete lesson inside a dropdown, republish the book, or produce full audio readings of his lesson text without written permission from the relevant rights holder.

How to use the two resources

Move out—and come back.

3

4
Reread Father Most

See whether the original Latin has become easier to notice and understand.

A practical limitation of the prototype

The external scan may not be the easiest legal route for every reader. Before public launch, TLE needs a properly checked list of purchase, borrowing and authorised online-access options for the exact edition being followed.

The project may later seek permission to display or record more of the course, but no approach will be made without Alan’s explicit approval.
