Source policy

How sources are used here.

The guide exists to help readers reach the Church’s own words more intelligently, not to replace them. So each kind of source is labelled distinctly.

Practical rule

When a free official source exists, the page should point to it plainly.

Church teaching

Catechism, councils, magisterial texts and liturgical books.

Church law

The Code of Canon Law and related legal materials.

Official communication

Holy See and bishops’ conference news or explanatory material.

Scholarship

Serious historical or academic work.

Notebook

The author’s own reflections, notes and aids.

Why this matters.

Readers should always be able to tell what the Church teaches, what historians argue, what canon law states and what is simply a lay person’s attempt to understand or explain.

Official sources first.

The majority of the site should nudge the reader towards the Catechism, Scripture, the councils and other official documents. Commentary is there to help, but not to hide the originals.
