Before Catholicism

Why does the question of God persist?

The word “God” can suggest a powerful being somewhere beyond the clouds. Classical Catholic thought is making a more fundamental claim.

Look

Notice the picture hidden inside the word “God”

Many arguments imagine God as one object inside the universe: invisible, extremely powerful and difficult to locate. Rejecting that picture may be sensible. It may also leave the classical Catholic claim untouched.

Ask

Is God one thing—or the source of there being anything at all?

Catholic theology does not present God as part of nature. It presents God as the source of existence: not another item requiring space, but the reason there is a universe, an order and anything capable of being known.

Understand

The question is philosophical before it is devotional

Why is there something rather than nothing? Why is reality intelligible? Can contingent things—things that need not have existed—fully explain their own existence?

Arguments from change, causation, contingency, order and moral experience try to reason from features of reality towards their deepest explanation. They are not laboratory tests and they remain open to criticism.

Check

Weigh

The strongest objection is not a clever slogan

Suffering and evil place pressure on belief in a good and powerful God. Any account that treats cruelty, disease, disaster and unanswered prayer as a footnote has failed to take the question seriously.

Catholicism answers through creation, freedom, the Cross, resurrection and final justice. Whether that answer is adequate will need its own chapter.

Try

Define the claim before accepting or rejecting it

  1. Write one sentence describing what you currently mean by “God”.
  2. Ask whether your sentence describes an object in the universe or the source of existence.
  3. List one reason the question seems meaningful and one reason it seems doubtful.

Review

The Catholic claim is not that God fills a gap in scientific knowledge. It is that every reality, known or unknown, depends upon the one who gives existence.
