Why does the question of God persist?

If religion were only a passing mistake, the question of God might have disappeared. Instead it keeps returning through wonder, conscience, beauty, suffering, mortality and the limits of explanation.

Look

What is already on the table?

The question appears whenever people confront existence itself: why there is something rather than nothing, why beauty wounds us, why conscience accuses us, why death matters, why the human mind reaches beyond immediate survival.

Ask

The question underneath

The persistence of the God-question does not prove that God exists, but it does suggest that the question is not absurd. Why does it recur so stubbornly?

Understand

The plain answer

Catholic thought says these are not full proofs by themselves, but genuine openings. The Church holds that human beings are capable of coming to some knowledge of God through reason, even though revelation is needed for deeper knowledge of who God is.

Weigh

Keep the complication visible

A fair objection

Many people experience beauty or conscience without seeing any reason to speak of God.

A Catholic reply

The Church does not say every person must make the same inference immediately. It says these are intelligible roads by which reason may be led towards God.

Review

The God-question persists because human life keeps pressing beyond mere mechanism: towards meaning, cause, value, beauty and destiny.
