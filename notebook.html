The author’s working notebook

Learning in public, with the labels left on.

These entries record reading, language study and developing questions. They are personal work—not Church teaching—and each strand points towards sources that can correct or deepen it.

Reading

Augustine · Confessions

Why write a confession addressed to God?

My first question was whether the book was a private sacramental confession made public. It is not. The work is prayer composed for human readers: memory, sin, praise and conversion interpreted before God.

Next task: trace the meanings of confessio and compare my first reading with reliable Augustinian scholarship and Church sources.

Saints

History · Hagiography

How do you read a saint without turning a life into a legend?

A saint’s life may reach us through devotion, later storytelling, historical record and official recognition. Those are related but not identical forms of evidence.

Next task: build a repeatable saint profile separating documented life, devotional tradition, cause of canonisation, local memory and what the Church proposes for imitation.
