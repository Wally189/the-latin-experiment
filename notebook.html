Reading · Augustine’s Confessions

Why write a confession addressed to God?

A first-time reader may expect a private sacramental confession made public. The work is something stranger: prayer addressed to God, composed for human readers, in which memory becomes theology.

The reader overhears a life being interpreted before God. That may explain why the prose feels both intensely personal and deliberately rhetorical: Augustine is not merely listing events; he is asking what a human life means when remembered in the presence of God.

Source work: distinguish the literary and theological senses of confessio, then compare this reading with reliable Augustinian scholarship.

Liturgy · Mass

Why ritual can appear empty before its grammar is understood

For a newcomer, the Mass can appear to assume knowledge: gestures, responses, silences and movements already understood by everyone else. It is reasonable to ask whether repetition has become mere habit.

Repeated attendance can alter the perception. What seemed like exclusion may become a stable form into which the individual is received. The interesting question is how shared ritual carries meaning without depending entirely on private emotion.

To develop: a visual guide to the shape of the Mass, its biblical language and the distinction between explanation and mystery.

Project method

Evangelisation without salesmanship

The site does not need to treat every visitor as a prospect moving through a conversion funnel. A more credible witness may be to take Catholicism seriously enough to examine it: sources read, claims distinguished, prayers learned and failures admitted.

Trust comes before instruction. Evangelisation, where it occurs, is the consequence of making the faith visible as coherent, demanding and alive.
