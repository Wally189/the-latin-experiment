Is faith opposed to science?

For many modern readers, this is the test question. If faith and science must fight, many will choose science before the discussion begins.

Look first

The page in one sentence

Science and theology both seek truth, but they do not always ask the same kind of question. Conflict arises most sharply when one is expected to do the work of the other.

Look

What is already on the table?

Science studies the natural world through observation, measurement, experiment and theory. Theology asks about God, creation, meaning, revelation and final purpose. They overlap in the same reality, but not always in the same register.

Ask

The question underneath

Science and theology both seek truth, but they do not always ask the same kind of question. Conflict arises most sharply when one is expected to do the work of the other.

Understand

The plain answer

Catholicism does not ask science to stop being science. It asks that science not pretend to be the only road to truth. The Church has a long intellectual tradition that includes scientific work, but it also insists that questions of meaning, purpose and God are not dissolved by physics or biology.

Weigh

Keep the complication visible

A fair objection

The Church’s history includes episodes where Christians have resisted scientific developments or read Scripture too crudely.

A Catholic reply

That history has to be faced honestly. But such failures do not amount to a doctrine that science is false or dangerous as such.

Try

Do something with it

  • Read Gaudium et Spes 36 and summarise it in your own words.
  • Identify one question science answers well and one question it cannot answer by experiment alone.

Review

Science and faith need not be rivals. They become rivals chiefly when one is made to occupy the whole field of truth.
