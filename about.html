The person inside the experiment

I am learning this, not teaching from a pedestal.

My name is Alan Gallagher. I am a British-Irish Catholic, a complete Latin beginner and somebody who has always been interested in the life, history and language of the Church.

Why I began

I have no wish to turn Latin into a badge of belonging or a verdict on how other Catholics worship. I simply keep encountering a language that sits beneath so much of the Western Church—and I want to understand it.

Latin is commonly called dead, yet it continues to appear in prayer, sacred music, official texts, theology, law, art and inscriptions. My small contribution to its life is to try to learn it properly, in public, and perhaps make another ordinary person think: I could try that.

What I am not claiming

  • I am not a Latin teacher or linguist.
  • I am not speaking for the Catholic Church.
  • I am not presenting personal preference as doctrine.
  • I am not hiding mistakes to create an image of effortless expertise.
  • I am not attempting to replace Father Most’s course.
A British-Irish Catholic viewpoint

Universal foundation, local voice.

The project begins with the wider Church and the broader Latin tradition, but its language and experience are recognisably British and Irish. Where local terminology, history or practice matters, it will be labelled as such rather than presented as universally binding.

The tone should be moderate, curious and intelligent: interested in the Church without becoming factional, and willing to explain a difficulty without pretending every question is a crisis.

The creative aim

I admire communicators who make familiar subjects newly interesting without making them silly: the spirit of Hannah Fry, Mary Beard or Brian Cox rather than a devotional pamphlet or an academic gatekeeping exercise.

How the work is made

Personal authorship, assisted production.

My contribution

The purpose, personal reflections, judgements, learning experience, tone and final approval belong to me. The first-person journal must arise from what I actually experienced.

AI assistance

AI helps research, organise, draft, code and identify questions. Its work must be checked against sources and may be wrong. It must not invent my spiritual or learning experience.
