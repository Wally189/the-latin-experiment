A lay convert’s continuing formation

I became Catholic.
Now I am beginning again.

For years I lived in practical agnosticism. Then I read, attended Mass, argued with what I found, and eventually received Baptism and Confirmation. This is a lifelong attempt to understand why I said yes.

The journey so far

Before

Living as though the question could wait

Not a settled philosophical rejection of God, but a life in which belief made little practical difference.

Encounter

Reading, Mass and an unexpected seriousness

Catholicism became harder to dismiss once it was encountered on its own terms rather than through assumptions.

Conflict

Questions before commitment

The path to Baptism and Confirmation was not frictionless. Difficult teachings and questions had to be faced rather than hidden.

Now

Beginning again from the foundations

Reception into the Church was a beginning. The work now is prayer, study, memory, conversion and ordinary fidelity.

The experiment

What happens when an ordinary person formed by a secular world decides to keep taking the Catholic faith seriously?

This site records the attempt. It is personal writing, not an official teaching authority. Where the Church teaches authoritatively, the source should be made clear.
