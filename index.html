Welcome to The Latin Experiment.

A learning journey through Father William Most’s Latin by the Natural Method: reading, handwriting, speaking, listening and honest reflection as the lessons are completed.

I’m Alan, an ordinary Catholic learner testing whether I can learn Ecclesiastical Latin using Father Most’s natural method. You are welcome to watch the experiment unfold, follow along with the lessons, and perhaps try it for yourself.

Choose how many days each week you can study consistently. The aim is not to rush through a lesson, but to give it enough attention to read, write, listen, speak and return to anything that did not settle the first time.

The practical kit

What you actually need

No specialist equipment and no paid subscription are required.

One ruled exercise book

An ordinary A4 or A5 ruled notebook is enough. A margin is useful for corrections; paper thick enough to write on both sides is a bonus, not a requirement.

Your phone’s voice recorder

Use the recorder already installed on the phone. Record in a quiet room, keep the phone the same distance away each time, and save the first and second readings separately.

L01-text-name-first-readingL01-text-name-second-reading

Optional, not essential

Headphones make comparisons easier. A pencil, one pen and a single highlighter are plenty. Avoid building a stationery project instead of learning Latin.

The useful missing item is a routine, not another purchase: book open, notebook ready, recorder available, 30–45 focused minutes. That is the complete working setup.
