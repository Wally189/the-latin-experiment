Catholicism, looked at again

What is Catholicism actually?

Most of us inherit an idea of Catholicism before we ever examine it: guilt, ritual, family memories, rules, scandal, perhaps beauty, perhaps anger. This project starts with those assumptions and looks more closely.

02

The method

Look before concluding.

Each subject begins where a reasonable sceptic might begin. The aim is not to win an argument, but to separate memory, caricature, history, doctrine and evidence.

  1. 01The familiar claimWhat people commonly understand or fear.
  2. 02Why it took holdHistory, experience, media, family memory or genuine failure.
  3. 03What Catholicism saysThe claim in the Church’s own words, not a softened substitute.
  4. 04What complicates itEvidence, disagreement, human failure and limits to easy answers.
  5. 05Why it mattersThe human question underneath the argument.

No ambush

You do not need to agree with Catholicism to examine it fairly.

The project is Catholic, but it begins by taking doubt, criticism and difficult history seriously. Personal writing is distinguished from the authoritative teaching of the Church, and sources should be visible enough for readers to check.
