Why this exists

A convert returning to the beginning.

The project is personal in origin, but the reader should not have to watch somebody else’s spiritual diary before finding an answer.

Practical agnosticism

Not a settled proof that God could not be known, but a life in which the question made little practical difference.

Before faith

I approached Catholicism from practical agnosticism. Reading and attending Mass brought me into contact with claims that were harder to dismiss once they were understood on their own terms. There were conflicts and hesitations before Baptism and Confirmation.

After reception

Becoming Catholic did not complete the learning. It created a lifelong responsibility to understand what I had professed, return to what had been forgotten and allow belief to affect prayer and ordinary life.

Why make it public?

Many people inherit fragments rather than the whole: a family grievance, a school memory, a scandal, a rule, a beautiful building or an argument from another century. The project tries to assemble the larger picture without hiding the failures.

Original work and future income

The author’s learning may eventually produce original guides, worksheets, audio, maps, timelines and reading companions. Any paid material must remain clearly separate from the Church’s teaching authority, and official sources should never be hidden behind a paywall.

The editorial promise

The site is Catholic and does not disguise Catholic teaching to make it easier to accept. It also does not present preference as doctrine, use controversy to generate attention or recruit readers into an internal faction.
