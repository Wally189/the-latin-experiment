Why this exists

A convert returning to the beginning.

The project is personal in origin, but it is not meant to make the visitor watch somebody else’s spiritual diary.

Practical agnosticism

Not a settled proof that God could not be known, but a life in which the question made little practical difference.

Before faith

I approached Catholicism from practical agnosticism. I read, attended Mass and encountered claims that were harder to dismiss once they were understood on their own terms. There were conflicts and hesitations before Baptism and Confirmation.

After reception

Becoming Catholic did not complete the learning. It created a lifelong obligation to understand what I had professed, to return to what had been forgotten, and to allow belief to affect prayer and ordinary life.

Why make it public?

Many people have inherited fragments of Catholicism rather than the whole: a family grievance, a school memory, a scandal, a rule, a beautiful building or an argument from another century. The project tries to assemble the larger picture without hiding the failures.

The editorial promise

The site is Catholic and does not disguise Catholic teaching to make it easier to accept. It also does not present personal preference as doctrine, use controversy to generate attention, or recruit readers into an internal faction.

Where the Church teaches authoritatively, the source should be visible. Where history is disputed, the disagreement should be acknowledged. Where the writing is personal, it should say so.
