My story

From practical agnosticism to faith

I did not begin with a dramatic rejection of God. I simply lived as though the question could remain indefinitely unanswered.

A working phrase

Practical agnosticism: not necessarily a firm conviction that God cannot be known, but a way of living in which the question of God has little practical effect.

The question became harder to ignore

I began to read about Catholicism and to attend Mass. What I encountered did not immediately resolve every difficulty. It created new ones. Catholic claims about truth, authority, worship, morality and the Church demanded more than vague spiritual sympathy.

There were conflicts before Baptism and Confirmation. Some were intellectual; others were personal and practical. The important point is not that every tension vanished. It is that Catholicism became sufficiently compelling that indifference was no longer an honest response.

Baptism and Confirmation were a beginning

Reception into the Church did not make formation complete. It established a direction and a lifelong obligation: to learn what I had professed, to pray, to receive the sacraments, to repent, and to let belief affect ordinary life.

Much can be forgotten after the concentrated preparation that precedes reception. Some things are learned only partially. Other questions emerge later. This project is therefore an act of return: beginning again with the foundations and asking why they matter.

Why publish the journey?

This is not a claim to teach with the authority of the Church. It is a lay person’s record of learning. Personal reflections will be identified as such. Statements of Catholic doctrine should be grounded in the Catechism, Scripture, councils, papal teaching and other authoritative sources.

The hope is that an honest account may be useful to another person living in a secular world: someone curious, doubtful, newly received, returning after an absence, or simply aware that their formation has gaps.

I made the commitment deliberately. Now I want to spend the rest of my life understanding it properly.

What comes next

The central strand is Beginning Again, a lay formation path followed in public. Alongside it sit the things that deepen and humanise the journey: prayer, ecclesiastical Latin, Augustine, saints, history, churches, books and the ordinary work of living as a Catholic.
