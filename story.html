About

Why this project exists.

The front door of the site is not autobiographical. It begins with the reader’s questions. But the project is still being made by a particular lay Catholic with a particular path into the Church.

The short version

A convert began learning in public and found that what was needed was neither a devotional blog nor an apologetics bunker, but a guide that kept opening into the Church’s own sources.

A practical agnostic, then a convert.

The project grows out of a lay conversion story: reading, attending Mass, wrestling with objections, then continuing to learn after Baptism and Confirmation rather than pretending the questions were finished.

Why not just a personal blog?

Because the site is meant first for the reader: the curious outsider, the returning Catholic, the cautious sceptic, the person who has inherited fragments. Personal notebook material remains visible, but never confuses itself with the Church’s teaching.

What it should become.

Over time the site should gather guided lessons, Latin helps, saint studies, book notes, timelines, maps and other resources that remain rooted in official Catholic sources where appropriate.
